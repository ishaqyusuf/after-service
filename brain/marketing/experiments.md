## Current Experiment Log

### 2026-05-29: Repair Shops Issue Resolution Workflow
Status: draft ready.

Audience:
- Repair shops.

Hypothesis:
- Repair shops have concrete post-repair moments where unresolved issues, repeat visits, and customer confidence depend on someone remembering to check back after the work leaves the shop.

Learning Goal:
- Understand how repair shops currently manage issue-resolution follow-up after a completed repair.

Channels:
- X/Twitter.
- LinkedIn.

Drafts:

X/Twitter:
```text
Question for repair shops:

After a repair leaves the shop, how do you make sure the customer is checked on if the issue comes back or they have questions?

Ticket note, spreadsheet, calendar, phone list, memory?

Building afterservice prelaunch; looking for real workflows.
```

LinkedIn:
```text
I'm looking for feedback from repair shop operators while building afterservice prelaunch.

After a completed repair leaves the shop, how do you make sure the customer gets checked on if the issue comes back, they have a question, or the repair needs another look?

Is that tracked in your POS, a ticket note, spreadsheet, calendar, phone list, WhatsApp/text thread, or mostly staff memory?

I'm trying to understand the real issue-resolution workflow before shaping the product too much. Public replies are useful, and DMs are welcome if the details are easier to share privately.
```

Expected Learning:
- Whether issue-resolution follow-up is owned by technicians, front-desk staff, or the owner.
- Which tool or habit records post-repair concerns.
- Whether repair shops distinguish review requests from issue-resolution check-ins.
