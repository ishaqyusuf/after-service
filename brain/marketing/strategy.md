# Marketing Strategy

## Purpose
This file is the source of truth for afterservice prelaunch marketing strategy.

## Current Stage
- Stage: prelaunch.
- Primary goal: create conversations with local service operators.
- Secondary goal: learn how operators currently handle post-service follow-up.
- Non-goal: polished launch selling before product feedback is stronger.

## Ideal Customer Profile
afterservice is for local service operators whose customer relationship continues after a job, appointment, installation, or visit is complete.

Priority segments:
- Repair shops.
- Salons and spas.
- Clinics and wellness operators.
- Local contractors.
- Installers.
- Field service teams.

## Positioning
afterservice helps local operators turn completed service work into structured customer check-ins, review requests, issue resolution, referrals, and repeat-business prompts.

## Core Problem
Local operators often lose revenue and trust after work is complete because follow-ups are scattered across memory, notebooks, calendars, spreadsheets, WhatsApp, email, or staff habits.

## Messaging Rules
- Keep tone plain, founder-led, and feedback-seeking.
- Ask about real workflows before pitching the product promise.
- Use concrete operator categories so the right people self-identify.
- Prefer questions that reveal current behavior, not abstract opinions.
- Avoid sounding like a launch announcement unless launch status changes in Brain.

## Channel Rules
- X/Twitter: short, specific, conversational, and under 280 characters.
- LinkedIn: slightly more context-rich, professional, and still feedback-first.
- Both channels should share one daily learning goal but use native wording.

## CTA Rules
- Primary CTA: ask people to reply publicly.
- Secondary CTA: invite DMs when the topic may involve private business details.
- Tertiary CTA: ask for a short call only when a reply shows strong fit or urgency.

## Daily Automation Cadence
- Run daily at 9 AM Africa/Lagos.
- Read Brain marketing docs before drafting.
- Recommend the day's audience, angle, and question.
- Draft one X post and one LinkedIn post for human approval.
- Ask for outcomes from yesterday's posts.
- Record important outcomes, decisions, drafts, and learnings in Brain.

## Human Approval Policy
- The automation must never auto-post.
- The human reviews, edits if needed, and manually posts.
- The human can paste post links, replies, DMs, or notes back into the thread for recording.

## Success Signals
- Replies from local operators describing current follow-up workflows.
- DMs or comments with specific tools, pain points, or missed opportunities.
- Repeated language that improves positioning or product scope.
- Operators agreeing to deeper conversations or beta feedback.

## Review Rhythm
- After 3 daily runs, review whether questions are producing useful replies.
- Promote high-performing drafts to `brain/marketing/content-bank.md`.
- Update this strategy only when a pattern repeats or the user makes a clear decision.
